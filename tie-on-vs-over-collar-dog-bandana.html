Construction first

Tie-on dog bandana vs. over-collar styles: what changes?

The two styles attach differently, so they are measured and handled differently. Start by identifying the construction rather than assuming one brand's size label matches another.

Side-by-side

The practical differences

Tie-on square

A square folds into a triangle and the ends form a removable knot. The fold and knot use part of the original fabric length, so compare the finished square measurement and allow room for tying.

Over-collar channel

A sewn channel slides over a separate collar. Check both the channel opening and the collar hardware dimensions; a neck measurement alone does not prove compatibility.

Adjustment

A tie-on style changes with the fold and knot. An over-collar style follows the adjustment range and width of the separate collar passing through it.

For this product

Pet Parade is a tie-on square

Pet Parade is configured as a square bandana in S 17 3/8 in (44 cm), M 21 1/4 in (54 cm), and L 25 1/4 in (64 cm), measured before folding and tying. It does not include an over-collar channel.

Use check

Keep the accessory role clear

Either style is an accessory and does not replace a collar, identification, harness, or restraint. Use supervised wear and remove the bandana if it shifts, catches, becomes damaged, or appears to bother the dog.
