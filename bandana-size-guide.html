Bandana size guide: S, M, or L?

Bandana labels are not universal. Start with the actual square dimensions, then consider how much fabric your fold and knot will use.

A practical comparison

Choose for the way you plan to wear it.

01

Start with a square you own

Lay a bandana flat and measure one edge. Compare that number with 44, 54, and 64 cm instead of relying on its old size label.

02

Allow for the fold

A diagonal fold changes the usable shape, and every knot consumes fabric. If your reference bandana only just ties, compare against the next larger square.

03

Do not treat S as adult-sized

The supplier explicitly reserves the smallest size for small pets. Adults should compare M and L against a bandana they already wear comfortably.
